# Have you ever experienced or witnessed great teamwork and professionalism? What made it effective?

Yes, I have. Working with some of my fellow classmates who were able to communicate, share ideas, and collaborate is what really made an effective team.

# What steps can you take to make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive?

What I would do is make sure my tone is respectful and clear on what message I want to get across. Additionally, I want to give them an opportunity to share their side or insights on the matter, fostering a back-and-forth dialogue.

# How can you help create a positive, fun, and professional work environment?

I think the best way to do it is to be casual and not too tense. Establish a good connection with your peers and engage in positive conversations. It's also best not to ride with workplace gossip especially if it doesn't set a good example. Always vouch for meaningful, positive, and helpful interactions with your workmates.

# How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?

It is best to prepare your materials needed prior to the meeting. You should know what you need to discuss and be ready to take notes. If you have a team, discuss with them prior to the meeting about the materials so that everyone will be on the same page going in.

# What would it look like to be proactive during your internship?

To be proactive during the internship is to not be afraid to ask questions when confused and seek to learn from your supervisors. If you have ideas to share, it is good to give your thoughts on the matter since it shows that you are a participant in the team.

# How might you follow up with colleagues or supervisors if you need information/action from them? How would you escalate it further if you don't get a response and when would you escalate?

It is good to email or send them a message about it first. A few follow-ups can be done for them to be reminded. However, if there is no reply and it is urgent, you may escalate by bypassing the email route and go for other means of communication, like messaging, texting, or calls when the need arises.

# What are some things you won't do?

The things I won't do is wait around to be instructed, be unprepared, not taking notes, ask irrelevant questions, not giving an update, ignore feedback, and being unclear with intentions regarding meetings and communication.
