# Other Productivity and Focus Apps

- **Freedom**: A utility-focused app and website blocker that is simple to use but limited to blocking distractions without helping build positive habits.
- **Cold Turkey**: It is a blocker primiarly for desktop devices. It is difficult to bypass, making it a good procastination blocker but lacks holistic lifestyle features.
- **Tiimo**: A highly visual daily planner designed specifically for neurodivergent users. It highights visual scheduling but does not physically block distractions.
- **Routinery**: A habit-tracking app focused on arranging micro-habits for morning and evening routines but does not offer the feature to block distractions.

# What makes Focus Bear different from these apps?

Focus bear offers a hybrid solution. It combines strict blocking functionality like that of Freedom and Cold Turkey, as well as routine-building and habit-tracking like that of Tiimo and Routinery. Instead of treating the symptom of distraction by simply blocking apps alone, Focus Bear also incorporates routine-based solutions that attempt to treat the root cause of distractions by enforcing healthy habits throughout the day.

# If you were a user, why would you choose Focus Bear over competitors?

I would choose Focus Bear because of the holistic approach the app is going for. This app can actively guide me through routines before starting my workday, which is helpful for building momentum on top of the distraction blocking. It is with Focus Bear I get to have a little bit of everything from the apps mentioned above.

# What’s one feature that other apps have that Focus Bear doesn’t?

One feature that Tiimo has over Focus Bear is the highly visual and aesthetic timelines. Additionally, Cold Turkey offers nuclear locking mechanisms of distractions, which seem to be much stricter than Focus Bear.

# Based on your research, what’s one improvement you think Focus Bear could make?

One improvement I can say would be a more streamlined onboarding and user interface. Because Focus Bear is trying to do a little bit of everything from the aformentioned apps, the learning curve is slightly steeper than a simpler app like Freedom. Improving the interface to be more visually intuitive can help new users bypass the overwhelming feeling of the app. Moreover, first time tutorials or walkthroughs when the user opens the app for the first time can also help.
